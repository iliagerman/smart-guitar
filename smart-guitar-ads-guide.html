Before You Start — Technical Setup

Complete these one-time setup steps before creating any ads. Skipping these means Meta can't track your results.

1

Verify Your Meta Pixel Is Installed

Your Pixel ID is 547626808918712. Before anything else, confirm it's firing correctly on your website.

  1. Install the Meta Pixel Helper Chrome extension from the Chrome Web Store
  2. Visit https://app.smart-guitar.com and https://smart-guitar.com
  3. Click the Pixel Helper icon — it should show a green checkmark and list PageView as firing
  4. Go through the full signup flow yourself. You should see these events fire in order: PageView → Lead → CompleteRegistration
⚠

Critical: The CompleteRegistration event must fire when the user submits the signup form — NOT after they confirm their email. If someone signs up from an Instagram ad, they're in Instagram's in-app browser. When they switch to their email app, the session breaks and Meta loses the data.

2

Configure Pixel Settings in Events Manager

  1. Go to Events Manager → select your Pixel → Settings
  2. Set "Track events automatically without code" → OFF (prevents ghost events that confuse the algorithm)
  3. Set "Automatic advanced matching" → ON (improves how well Meta matches visitors to profiles)
  4. Under Traffic permissions allow list, add: smart-guitar.com and app.smart-guitar.com
3

Deploy Conversions API (CAPI)

Ad blockers destroy 20–30% of your pixel data. Server-side event tracking ensures Meta gets clean, complete data.

  1. In Events Manager, go to Settings → Conversions API
  2. Follow Meta's setup guide to deploy a server-side container (AWS or your own server)
  3. Send these events server-to-server: CompleteRegistration, Subscribe, PlaySong
  4. Use the same event_id in both browser pixel and server events so Meta can deduplicate them
💡

This is technical — if you're not comfortable, ask your developer to handle it. The key point: both your browser pixel AND your server should send the same events with matching event_id values.

4

Test Everything in Events Manager

  1. Go to Events Manager → Test Events
  2. Enter your website URL and click Open Website
  3. Walk through the signup flow on the opened page
  4. Come back to Test Events — you should see events appearing in real time
  5. Confirm: PageView, Lead, and CompleteRegistration all show up

Phase 1 — Foundation & Alpha Testing (Weeks 1–4)

5

Create the Campaign in Ads Manager

Open Meta Ads Manager (adsmanager.facebook.com) and follow these exact steps:

  1. Click the green + Create button
  2. Select Leads as your campaign objective → click Continue
  3. Choose Manual Leads Campaign (do NOT use the simplified/smart setup)
  4. Name the campaign: Smart Guitar - Leads - Broad - Canada
  5. Turn Advantage Campaign Budget (CBO) → ON
  6. Set daily budget: $15.00
  7. Click Next to proceed to Ad Set
💡

Why "Leads" and not "Sales"? At $6/month, you'll never generate enough purchases to feed Meta's algorithm. "Leads" with CompleteRegistration as the conversion event hits the sweet spot: high-intent signal that happens frequently enough for the algorithm to learn.

Filming Tips:

  1. Lighting: Film near a window during daytime. Natural light looks best. No overhead fluorescent.
  2. Audio: A quiet room is enough. No external mic needed, but avoid echo-y spaces.
  3. Camera: Hold your phone vertically. Front-facing camera (selfie mode).
  4. Speaking: Don't memorize the script word-for-word. Know the 3 beats (hook → explain → demo) and talk naturally.
  5. Captions: Add captions to the entire video. Use CapCut (free) or Meta's auto-caption feature in Ads Manager.
  6. Hook text: Add bold white text in the first 3 seconds. This is what stops people from scrolling.
  7. Screen recording: On iPhone: Settings → Control Center → add Screen Recording. On Android: swipe down → Screen Record.

10

Launch — Then Don't Touch Anything for 5 Days

Click Publish. Your ads will go into Meta's review queue (usually approved in 1–24 hours). Once live:

⛔

DO NOT edit anything, pause anything, or change the budget for 5 full days. Daily results will swing wildly. One day might show $2 per registration, the next $15. This is normal. Wait for the full picture.

⚠

You WILL see a "Learning Limited" warning. This is because $15/day won't hit 50 conversions/week. Ignore it completely. It does NOT mean your ads are broken. Judge by actual cost per registration and your own signup data.

11

After at least 7 days, check your numbers. Look in both Ads Manager AND your own database (the UTM tags make this possible).

Where to find these numbers: In Ads Manager, customize your columns to show: Impressions, Link CTR, Cost per Result (this is your Cost per CompleteRegistration), Amount Spent, and Results.

Copyright Safety — What You Can & Can't Show

Meta will reject your ads if copyrighted content is visible. Follow these rules strictly.

✅ DO

  • Use royalty-free music in video ads
  • Blur or use fictional song/artist names in screenshots
  • Show the app UI with generic or dummy data
  • Film yourself playing original riffs
  • Use testimonial clips of people talking

❌ DON'T

  • Show recognizable song titles or artist names
  • Play copyrighted songs audibly in ads
  • Show album artwork or cover art
  • Film yourself playing a recognizable hit song
  • Show YouTube or Spotify branding anywhere

7 Golden Rules — Tape These to Your Monitor

Break any of these and you'll waste budget. Seriously.

1. Ignore "Learning Limited" warnings. At $15/day, you won't hit 50 conversions/week. The warning is just a label — evaluate by your actual cost per registration.
2. Don't touch anything for 3–5 days after launch. Daily variance is huge. One bad day doesn't mean a bad ad.
3. Never increase budget by more than 20% at a time. Big jumps reset learning. Follow: $15 → $18 → $20.
4. Never pause a winning ad. Only kill the loser and introduce a new challenger.
5. One variable at a time. Don't change audience AND creative AND budget at the same time.
6. Check results weekly, not hourly. Meta's conversion reporting lags 24–72 hours.
7. Cross-reference with your own data. Compare Meta's numbers with actual signups in your database using the UTM tags.
